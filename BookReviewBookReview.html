"Top Rated and Expert Reviewed Books".

1. Long Island by Coim Tobin

Rating - ★★★★★

"Long Island" by Colm Tóibín, a celebrated author known for his evocative prose and deep exploration of human emotions, takes readers on a journey through the lives of its complex characters set against the backdrop of Long Island. This book is an exemplary work that showcases Tóibín's ability to weave intricate narratives with rich, atmospheric settings.

2. Real Americans by Rachel Khong

Rating - ★★★★

"Real Americans" by Rachel Khong is a poignant and insightful exploration of identity, belonging, and the intricacies of the American experience through the eyes of its diverse characters. Known for her keen observations and empathetic storytelling, Khong delivers a compelling narrative that resonates with contemporary themes.

3. Parade by Rachel Cusk

Rating - ★★★

Parade" by Rachel Cusk is a striking addition to her body of work, showcasing her signature style of introspective and finely crafted prose. Known for her keen insights into human nature and relationships, Cusk delivers a narrative that is both thought-provoking and deeply personal.

4. Horror Movie by Paul Tremblay

Rating - ★★★★★

"Horror Movie" by Paul Tremblay is a gripping addition to the horror genre, showcasing Tremblay's knack for blending psychological terror with supernatural elements. Known for his ability to delve into the minds of his characters and evoke genuine fear, Tremblay crafts a narrative that is both chilling and thought-provoking.

5. This Strange Eventful History by Claire Messud

Rating - ★★★★

"This Strange Eventful History" by Claire Messud is an intricate exploration of life's unpredictability and the profound impact of personal history on identity. Messud, known for her insightful character studies and elegant prose, delivers a narrative that is both deeply engaging and thought-provoking

6. The Friday Afternoon Club by Griffin Dunne

Rating - ★★★★★

"The Friday Afternoon Club" by Griffin Dunne is a compelling and heartwarming exploration of friendship, resilience, and the complexities of modern life. Known for his storytelling prowess, Dunne weaves a narrative that is both engaging and emotionally resonant.

7. All Fours by Miranda July

Rating - ★★★★★

"All Fours" by Miranda July is a novel that will spark conversation. If you're looking for something out of the ordinary and are open to a frank exploration of female experience, this book might be for you. However, if you prefer a more traditional narrative or are easily disturbed by sexual content, you might want to choose something else.

8. Knife by Salman Rushdie

Rating - ★★★★

Knife" is a powerful and moving read. It's a testament to the human spirit's ability to overcome adversity and a reminder of the importance of free expression. However, be prepared for the book's intensity and its unflinching portrayal of trauma.

9. Travelling by Ann Powers

Rating - ★★★

"Travelling" is a must-read for any Joni Mitchell fan. It's a refreshing take on the biographical genre, offering a nuanced and insightful portrait of a musical icon. However, even those unfamiliar with Mitchell will appreciate Powers' beautiful prose and the book's exploration of artistic expression, self-discovery, and the enduring power of music.

10. The Man Who Pays The Rent by Judi Dench

Rating - ★★★★★

"The Man Who Pays the Rent" is a charming and informative read for anyone who appreciates Shakespeare or is interested in the world of theatre. Dench's passion is contagious, and her insights offer a valuable perspective on the enduring power of Shakespeare's work.

11. The God Of The Woods by Liz Moore

Rating - ★★★★

Liz Moore's "The God of the Woods" is a captivating novel that weaves a tale of suspense, family drama, and hidden truths. Set against the backdrop of a seemingly idyllic summer camp, the story delves into the complexities of human relationships and the enduring power of the past.

12. Enlightenment by Sarah Perry

Rating - ★★★★★

Enlightenment is a thought-provoking novel that will appeal to readers who enjoy literary fiction with rich themes and complex characters. However, those seeking a fast-paced plot or a clear resolution to the central conflict might find it slow-moving.
